01 — Members

Getting more people through the door.

A visitor who doesn't know what to do next doesn't become a member. Every part of this section exists to remove a hesitation.

Trial booking, everywhere

Nav, hero, and every class card lead to the same booking flow. No dead ends, no separate contact page to hunt for.

Referral tracking

A simple "how did you hear about us" question on the trial form. The gym now has real data on which channel actually brings people in, something it never had before.

"Which class is right for you?"

One click answers the single most common hesitation for a first-time visitor, before they've read a word of the schedule.

A visible path forward

Beginner, intermediate, competition, shown as a ladder, not implied. People stay longer when they can see where they're headed.

Bilingual, properly

Every section works in Swedish and English from one system. Stockholm's English-speaking population was simply invisible to the old site.

Honest, not oversold

The schedule has no "book this class" button on purpose. Masters Gym is drop-in, so a booking button would promise something the club can't actually deliver. Trust matters more than looking feature-complete.

Meeting members where they already look

Instagram, Facebook and TikTok are one click away from the nav and the footer, not buried on a separate contact page.

Dark or light, visitor's choice

A small detail most competitors skip, but it's the kind of polish that signals a site was actually considered, not templated.

02 — Money

Protecting and growing revenue.

Some of this is about earning more. Some of it is about no longer quietly losing what was already there.

Membership clarity

Every tier, every price, a call to action next to each one. No visitor has to ask how to actually sign up.

Corporate leads that self-qualify

A dedicated section spells out exactly what's included and promises a quote within 24 hours, instead of one paragraph and a dead link.

A real leak, found and fixed

The old booking form could show "sent!" even when the submission silently failed. That's not a cosmetic bug, that's trial requests disappearing with nobody noticing. Fixed.

Findable, not just visible

Structured data tells Google exactly what the business is and where it is. Being easy to find is directly worth money for a local business.

03 — Admin

Time given back.

The old site's real problem was never that a developer had to do everything. It was that the tools didn't fit how the club actually works.

News the club publishes itself

A real CMS, with photo support, replacing pages that had to be built one at a time outside the site's own navigation.

A schedule that fits how they work

Kept on a spreadsheet on purpose, genuinely the fastest way to edit fast-moving weekly class times, with checks so a typo can't break the page.

Season notes, without a developer

An optional "important information" field the club fills in themselves when something needs flagging, no code required.

Planned, not yet live

Automatic replies, ready to switch on

A trial request will soon trigger an automatic reply with what to bring, arrival time, and location, no waiting on a manual response.

04 — Built properly

The craft underneath.

Built to load fast, to work for people using a keyboard or a screen reader, and to actually get found on Google, not just look finished.

Every form field, every menu, every modal was checked for the kind of thing that's invisible until it breaks for someone.

  • Stack: hand-written HTML, CSS and vanilla JS, no framework, single static file, hosted on GitHub Pages.
  • Content layer: Sanity CMS via GROQ queries for news, published Google Sheets CSV endpoint for the schedule.
  • Performance: eliminated a render-blocking third-party stylesheet, preloaded the hero image, added font preconnects. Mobile Lighthouse performance went from unmeasurable to 91, desktop to 99.
  • Accessibility: modal focus trap with Escape-to-close and focus return, full ARIA labeling, keyboard-operable carousel, corrected heading hierarchy, restored pinch-to-zoom.
  • Security: innerHTML output escaped throughout, honeypot field on the trial form.
  • SEO: canonical URL, LocalBusiness structured data, sitemap.xml, robots.txt, branded 404 page.
